Infectious Diseases Overview

Comprehensive information about common infectious diseases, their symptoms, and prevention methods

COVID-19

Caused by: SARS-CoV-2 virus
Transmission: Respiratory droplets, airborne
Incubation: 2-14 days

Symptoms

  • Fever or chills
  • Cough and shortness of breath
  • Fatigue and muscle aches
  • Loss of taste or smell
  • Sore throat and headache
  • Nausea or diarrhea (less common)

Prevention

  • Vaccination and booster shots
  • Wearing masks in crowded places
  • Frequent hand washing with soap
  • Maintaining social distance
  • Proper indoor ventilation
  • Isolation when symptomatic

Influenza

Caused by: Influenza viruses
Transmission: Respiratory droplets
Incubation: 1-4 days

Symptoms

  • Sudden high fever
  • Muscle and joint pain
  • Headache and fatigue
  • Dry cough and sore throat
  • Runny or stuffy nose
  • Sometimes vomiting/diarrhea (in children)

Prevention

  • Annual flu vaccination
  • Avoid close contact with sick people
  • Cover coughs and sneezes
  • Stay home when symptomatic
  • Regular hand hygiene
  • Disinfect frequently touched surfaces

Dengue

Caused by: Dengue virus (DENV)
Transmission: Aedes mosquitoes
Incubation: 4-10 days

Symptoms

  • High fever (104°F/40°C)
  • Severe headache
  • Pain behind the eyes
  • Muscle and joint pain
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Skin rash (appears 2-5 days after fever)

Prevention

  • Mosquito control measures
  • Use of EPA-registered repellents
  • Wearing long-sleeved clothing
  • Eliminating standing water
  • Window and door screens
  • Avoiding outdoor activities at peak biting times

HMPV

Caused by: Human metapneumovirus
Transmission: Respiratory droplets
Incubation: 3-6 days

Symptoms

  • Cough and nasal congestion
  • Fever and sore throat
  • Shortness of breath
  • Wheezing (especially in children)
  • Pneumonia in severe cases
  • Acute bronchitis

Prevention

  • Frequent hand washing
  • Avoiding touching face with unwashed hands
  • Avoiding close contact with sick individuals
  • Covering coughs and sneezes
  • Disinfecting frequently touched surfaces
  • Staying home when sick
