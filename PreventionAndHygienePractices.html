Prevention & Hygiene Practices

Essential guidelines to protect yourself and your community

Personal Hygiene

Hand Hygiene

Wash with soap for 20 seconds
Clean between fingers and under nails
Use alcohol-based sanitizer when water unavailable
Always wash before eating and after restroom

Respiratory Hygiene

Cough/sneeze into elbow or tissue
Immediately dispose used tissues
Wear masks in crowded places
Maintain distance from sick individuals

Home Hygiene

Regularly disinfect high-touch surfaces
Ensure proper ventilation
Separate personal items during illness
Clean and cover wounds properly

Community Prevention

Vaccination

Stay up-to-date with recommended immunizations:

1 Consult local health authorities for recommended vaccines
2 Keep records of all vaccinations
3 Participate in community vaccination drives
4 Educate others about vaccine benefits

Sanitation

Proper waste management prevents disease spread:

1 Dispose garbage in covered bins
2 Eliminate standing water to prevent mosquito breeding
3 Properly manage human and animal waste
4 Participate in community clean-up efforts

Proper Handwashing Technique

1. Wet Hands
Use clean running water
2. Apply Soap
Enough to cover all surfaces
3. Scrub 20 Sec
All surfaces including nails
4. Rinse & Dry
Use clean towel or air dry

Hygiene Myths Debunked

Myth: Antibacterial soap is always better

Regular soap is equally effective for most daily hygiene needs when used properly.

Myth: You need to bathe multiple times daily

Over-bathing can dry skin and remove protective oils. Once daily is sufficient for most.

Additional Protection Tips

  • Practice food safety - cook thoroughly and store properly
  • Take precautions when traveling to high-risk areas
  • Use safe water sources and proper sanitation
  • Keep first aid kits stocked and accessible
